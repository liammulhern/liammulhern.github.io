Project: Formula 1 in Schools

Catergory: UI / UX / HTML / JS / CSS

Year: 2019

Overview:

Formula 1 in Schools is the only global multi-disciplinary challenge in which teams of students aged 9 to 19 deploy CAD/CAM software to collaborate, design, analyse, manufacture, test, and then race miniature, compressed air powered, balsa wood, F1 cars.

Role:

As lead graphic designer and assistant engineer my multifaceted role within team Hardwired was always intertwined with design. By creating our team’s identity

Design:

My journey with website development started here, where I was tasked with the responsibility of transferring our team’s identity to the internet and display our strengths to prospective sponsors. Utilising Adobe Illustrator to prototype the webpage and then building the site in Visual Studio Code, I was able to build an understanding of HTML, CSS and JavaScript whilst strengthening my UI/UX design skills.

During this time, I was given the opportunity to work with team sponsors to professionally develop my graphic design skills and learn the foundational principles of front-end development. Whilst my strengths lie in software, JavaScript offered the best of both worlds, where I could integrate lower-level APIs to display social media feeds and databases to easily update sponsor listings.
